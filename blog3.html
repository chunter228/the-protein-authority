Protein Myths Debunked: How Much Do You Really Need?

It’s easy to believe that more protein equals more muscle. In reality, muscle growth requires both adequate protein and strength training. Registered dietitians recommend about 0.36‑0.6 grams of protein per pound of body weight (roughly 50‑100 g per day for most adults)【568182238129158†L270-L332】【568182238129158†L334-L343】.

Professor Joanne Slavin explains that Americans aren’t generally protein‑deficient【639489652103378†L109-L156】. Piling on powder won’t transform your physique; quality, variety and timing matter more. Whole food sources like fish, poultry, legumes and nuts provide essential vitamins and minerals alongside amino acids.

The takeaway? Spread your protein across meals, pair it with carbohydrates and healthy fats, and don’t stress if you occasionally fall short. Your body is brilliant at recycling amino acids to meet daily needs.
